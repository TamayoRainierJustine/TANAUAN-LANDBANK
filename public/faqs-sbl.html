iAccess, weAccess, and Institutional (REG ACICDES)

Short process notes for presentations. For official details, use the official Terms & Conditions and manuals.

iAccess (Retail)

Internet banking facility for clients. May be unavailable during service periods/maintenance; may lock after 3 incorrect password attempts.

  • Enroll: Online enrollment or via branch submission (LIEMA/DOBS).
  • Security: Keep password/OTP confidential; OTP may be required for some transactions.
  • Downtime: Transactions after downtime/holidays post next banking day.
Terms & enrollment More info

weAccess (Institutional)

Internet banking for institutions with user roles (e.g., Corporate Admin, Maker, Authorizer). Some services may be unavailable during service periods.

  • Roles: Maker initiates, Authorizer approves, Corporate Admin manages users (or branch handles if none).
  • Access: Only enrolled deposit accounts and authorized users.
  • Controls: User IDs may be disabled after 3 wrong passwords; inactivity rules may apply.

Institutional: REG ACICDES

Stand-alone data entry system to facilitate processing of voluminous ACIC for Regular demand deposit accounts, with output files uploaded thru weAccess.

  • Purpose: Reduces manual encoding and supports ACIC file generation for upload.
  • Users: Agencies with Regular ACIC accounts enrolled in weAccess.
  • Flow: Encode → generate ACIC file → upload (weAccess ACIC module).

Q: Who is eligible to apply?
A: All existing MSME depositors (single proprietor, partnership or corporation) with an average daily balance (ADB) of at least ₱1.50 million for the last three (3) years may apply, subject to standard credit evaluation.

Q: How much is the loanable amount?
A: Minimum of ₱1.0 million, up to 90% of the client’s deposit ADB, but not to exceed ₱10.0 million.

Q: What are the available credit facilities?
A: There are two facilities: Revolving Credit Line (RCL), a one-year credit line with 90‑day promissory notes; and Short‑Term Loan (STL), a one‑shot loan payable in three (3) equal monthly installments.

Q: Can the loan be renewed if the borrower cannot pay on maturity?
A: No. There is no PN renewal or roll‑over under this facility. Loans must be paid on maturity date.

Reminder: Only existing depositors meeting the minimum ADB requirement are eligible under this facility.

Q: What is the mode of collection?
A: Payments are collected via Automatic Debit Arrangement (ADA) on the client’s existing deposit account with LandBank.

Q: What will be the collateral? Will the deposit be put on hold?
A: This facility is a clean / unsecured loan. The required deposit ADB is not put on hold and the client may still withdraw, but must maintain the required ADB equivalent to 100% of the outstanding principal balance.

Q: What happens if there are no available funds at maturity?
A: It is important that the required ADB is maintained to ensure source of repayment. The borrower’s deposit account will be debited for loan payment.

Q: Is there a factor rate for monthly amortization?
A: None. For RCL, principal is payable in lump sum at maturity; for STL, principal is payable in three equal monthly amortizations with PN term up to 90 days.

Q: How much is the interest rate?
A: Rates depend on credit scoring. A “low‑risk” score usually corresponds to about 9% p.a., while “normal‑risk” corresponds to about 10% p.a.

Q: How is interest computed?
A: Interest collected in advance may be computed as:
Interest = (Loan amount × applicable rate) × (PN term / 360).

Q: How are penalty charges computed?
A: Penalty may be computed as:
Penalty = (Unpaid amount × 24%) × (no. of days past due / 360), reckoned from the day after maturity up to actual settlement.

Q: Is cash flow still required if financial statements are submitted?
A: No, if FS are sufficient to cover the required debt service. Otherwise, additional cash‑flow schedules or bank statements for the last six (6) months may be requested.

Q: How and to whom should documentary requirements be submitted?
A: Requirements are submitted via Secure File Transfer Protocol (SFTP) or other designated platforms / email addresses of the lending units, depending on the branch location (NCR, Luzon, Visayas or Mindanao).

Q: What are the related fees and charges?
A: Fees typically include an application fee (around ₱2,000 for loans up to ₱10 million), processing / handling fee (per ₱1.0 million approved), commitment fee on unavailed amounts, notarial fees and applicable documentary stamp tax (DST).

Q: What if the depositor or borrower does not meet the required ADB or minimum loan amount?
A: If the client does not meet the ₱1.50 million ADB or needs less than ₱1.0 million, they may instead be referred to other SME lending units or other products.

Q: When can branches start endorsing applications?
A: Endorsement may begin once the formal guidelines are released, as indicated in the circular.

Note: Clients should coordinate with their servicing branch or relationship officer for the latest contact persons and submission channels.
