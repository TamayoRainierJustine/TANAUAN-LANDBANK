LANDBANK SMALL BUSINESS LOAN (SBL)

Eligibility & Loan Features

Eligibility
🧑‍💼
Existing MSME depositor
(single proprietor, partnership, or corporation)
📊
Average Daily Balance (ADB)
≥ ₱1.5M for the last 3 years
🏦
Maintained LANDBANK deposit account
used for SBL servicing while loan is outstanding
✅
Clean / unsecured facility
no real estate or chattel collateral required
Loan Features
💰
Loan amount range
₱1M – ₱10M
📈
Maximum based on deposits
up to 90% of ADB subject to credit evaluation
📄
Facilities
Revolving Credit Line (RCL) – 1 year via 90‑day PNs
Short‑Term Loan (STL) – payable in 3 monthly payments
📉
Interest rate (indicative)
9% – 10% p.a. depending on risk rating and internal pricing
🔁 Repayments are automatically collected via Automatic Debit Arrangement (ADA) from the client’s LANDBANK deposit account.

Deposit

Deposit & Income Relationship

Deposit behaviour and business performance

📋
Proof that the MSME depositor maintained the minimum ₱1.50 million deposit ADB for the last three (3) years.
📈
Confirmation that the business remains profitable and can meet debt‑service requirements.
📑
If financial statements are not sufficient, the bank may also request cash‑flow schedules or bank statements / passbooks for at least six (6) months.
🔄
For renewals or subsequent availments, updated financial statements and deposit behaviour may be evaluated.

Fees

Fees, Charges & Reminders

Based on the official SBL FAQs and annexes

🪙 Typical Fees
  • Application Fee: around ₱2,000 for loan amounts up to ₱10.0 million.
  • Processing Fee – Handling: ₱1,000 for every ₱1.0 million of approved loan amount.
  • Processing Fee – Commitment: ₱5,000 for every ₱1.0 million of unavailed portion of the approved line.
  • Notarial Fee: around ₱500 per document, plus Documentary Stamp Tax (DST) and other regulatory charges.
🔔 Penalties & Reminders
  • Penalty for past due amounts may be computed using the FAQs formula (unpaid amount × 24% × days past due / 360).
  • Eligibility under SBL is limited to existing depositors that meet the ADB requirement; other accounts may be referred to SME lending units.
  • Processing generally begins once complete documentary requirements are submitted through prescribed channels (SFTP or designated email addresses).
